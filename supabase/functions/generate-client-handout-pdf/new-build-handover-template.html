Your Consumer Unit
Your consumer unit (fuse board) is the central hub of your electrical system. It distributes power to every circuit and protects you from electrical faults. {% if cu_location %}It is located in the {{ cu_location }}{% endif %}
Main switch: The large switch (usually at the top or left) turns off all electricity to your home. In an emergency, switch this off first{% if main_switch_rating %} — rated at {{ main_switch_rating }}{% endif %}
RCDs / RCBOs: These are your life-saving devices. They detect faults and cut power in milliseconds to prevent electric shock. Your installation has RCBOs (individual protection per circuit) — if one trips, only that circuit is affected
SPD (Surge Protection Device): Your consumer unit includes an SPD that protects your home from voltage spikes caused by lightning strikes or grid surges. It protects all your connected electronics — TVs, computers, smart home devices
Metal enclosure: Modern consumer units are housed in a non-combustible metal enclosure as required by BS 7671, providing fire protection

Socket Safety — Getting the Most From Your Installation
One plug per socket. Your new sockets are rated at 13A (3,120W maximum). Avoid multi-way adapters — if you need more sockets in a location, contact us to add them properly
USB sockets: If USB charging sockets were installed, they provide 5V charging without needing a separate adapter. They are safe to leave energised when not charging a device
Outdoor sockets: Any outdoor sockets are weatherproof (IP66) and individually RCD-protected. Always close the cover when not in use. Use an RCD adapter for any outdoor power tools
Kitchen sockets: Positioned away from the sink and hob for safety. Worktop sockets are for portable appliances only — don't leave heavy appliances permanently connected to worktop outlets
Lighting — Tips for Your New System
LED bulbs: Your light fittings are designed for LED bulbs. When replacing, match the fitting type (GU10, B22 bayonet, E27 screw, etc.) and wattage. LED equivalents use 80-90% less energy than the old-style bulbs they replace
Dimmer switches: If dimmers are fitted, ensure replacement bulbs are "dimmable" — not all LEDs are. Non-dimmable LEDs on a dimmer will flicker or buzz
Bathroom lighting: Bathroom lights are controlled by pull-cord switches or wall switches outside the room. They are rated for wet environments (IP44 or higher). Do not install standard fittings in bathrooms
External lighting: PIR motion sensors are programmed to stay on for a set duration (typically 1-5 minutes). Sensitivity and duration can usually be adjusted — refer to the manufacturer's instructions or ask us
Smoke, Heat & CO Alarms
Your alarms are mains-wired with battery backup and interconnected — when one detects danger, all alarms in the house sound simultaneously
Smoke alarms are fitted in hallways and landings on every floor. Heat alarms are in the kitchen (they respond to temperature, not smoke, so no false alarms from cooking)
Carbon monoxide alarms (if fitted) are in rooms with gas appliances. CO is odourless — you cannot detect it without an alarm. If it sounds, open windows, leave the property, and call the gas emergency line: 0800 111 999
Test monthly by pressing and holding the test button until you hear the alarm. Replace the entire unit every 10 years — the sensor degrades over time even if the alarm still sounds when tested
What do the beeps mean?
Continuous loud alarm: Danger detected — evacuate immediately. Intermittent chirp every 30-60 seconds: Low battery — replace the backup battery now. Three beeps then silence: End of life — the whole unit needs replacing.
Earthing & Bonding — Your Safety System
Green and yellow labels reading "SAFETY ELECTRICAL CONNECTION — DO NOT REMOVE" are fitted to bonding connections on your gas and water pipes. Never remove, disconnect, or allow a plumber to disconnect these without consulting an electrician first
If a plumber works on your gas or water pipes, they may need to temporarily disconnect bonding. Ensure they reconnect it or contact {{ company_name }} to verify it has been properly reinstated

When Something Trips
Single RCBO tripped: Only one circuit is affected. Unplug everything on that circuit, reset the RCBO. Plug items back one at a time — the one that causes the trip is faulty
Main switch tripped: All power is off. Check with neighbours — if they're also out, it's a supply fault. Call 105 (free, 24/7). If it's just you, try resetting
Keeps tripping: Do NOT keep resetting. There is an active fault. Call {{ company_name }} on {{ company_phone }}
SPD indicator red: Your surge protection has absorbed a surge and needs replacing. Not urgent but book it soon — you're unprotected until replaced
Emergency Procedures
Electric Shock
Do NOT touch the person. Switch off at the consumer unit main switch. If you can't reach it safely, use a dry non-metallic object to separate them from the source. Call 999 immediately.
Electrical Fire
Never use water. Switch off at the consumer unit if safe to do so. Use a CO2 or dry powder extinguisher. If in doubt, get everyone out and call 999.
Burning Smell from Consumer Unit
Switch off the main switch immediately. Do not attempt to investigate. Call {{ company_name }} on {{ company_phone }} urgently. Do not switch back on until inspected.
What You Can Safely Do Yourself
Change light bulbs — switch off at the wall first, let the old bulb cool
Change a fuse in a plug — 3A for lamps and electronics, 13A for kettles, heaters, irons
Test RCDs and smoke alarms — press the test buttons as described in this guide
Reset a tripped RCBO — push the switch back up after identifying and removing the cause
Everything else needs a qualified electrician — adding sockets, moving switches, bathroom work, outdoor wiring, EV charger installation, replacing the consumer unit
