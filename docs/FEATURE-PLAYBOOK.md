## 2. Apprentice Hub

### The Today page + bottom tab bar (the daily front door)
**How it works:** Apprentices live on five thumb-tabs — **Today · Learn · CAPTURE · Hours · Me** — pinned to the bottom of every apprentice and Study Centre screen, with a raised yellow CAPTURE button in the middle. The Today page runs a priority chain each morning: overdue tutor quiz first, then new set work, then "you're behind your hours pace", then "keep the streak alive", then "start learning". Below it: a tappable stat strip (streak / week's OTJ hours / course % / evidence awaiting sign-off), four one-tap quick actions and the From-your-college card.
**The benefit:** An apprentice never has to *decide* what to do — the app triages their apprenticeship for them. College work can't be missed; hours can't silently drift behind.
**Sell it:** *"One screen that tells you exactly what your apprenticeship needs from you today — and one yellow button that captures site evidence in seconds."*

### Unified evidence capture with AI grading
**How it works:** Tap CAPTURE anywhere. Snap several site photos or attach documents in one go, then *dictate* what you did. The AI drafts a structured STAR reflection (Situation/Task/Action/Result/Learning), matches the evidence to the assessment criteria of the apprentice's actual qualification (first matches land in ~1.5 seconds), and grades each file **A–D** with concrete tips to strengthen it ("add the isolation photo", "name the test instrument") *before* it's filed. Drafts persist offline; failed uploads never lose the form.
**The benefit:** Portfolio evidence goes from "shoebox of photos the night before gateway" to graded, criteria-mapped entries captured in the moment. Tutors get better evidence; apprentices hit gateway with confidence.
**Sell it:** *"Take the photo, say what you did — your evidence is written up, matched to your criteria and graded A–D before you've left the job."*

### Portfolio dashboard
**How it works:** A tabbed workspace (Home / My Work / Progress / Me) organised around the qualification standard: every assessment criterion with status, a coverage heatmap, a smart "today's focus" ranking which criteria to capture next, an EPA-gateway readiness pulse, recent evidence with thumbnails, tutor sign-offs and comments, plus qualification switching and a shareable read-only portfolio view for gateway.
**The benefit:** The apprentice always knows the gap between where they are and gateway — and exactly which job tomorrow could close it.
**Sell it:** *"Your whole NVQ portfolio, live — with the app telling you which evidence to capture next."*

### Off-the-job hours (the defensibility engine)
**How it works:** The OTJ hub tracks against the apprenticeship standard's real fixed-hours requirement (e.g. 1,066 hours for ST0152 — not a vague 20%). Week and year totals, a verification rate, and a stacked **source-mix bar** showing where hours came from: auto-tracked in-app study, apprentice-submitted, tutor-recorded, employer-attested. A compliance forecast projects whether they'll clear gateway at the current pace and says how many hours a week closes the gap.
Logging is voice-first: say *"Tuesday afternoon, installed a consumer unit in a terraced house, about two hours"* — the parser fills title, duration, date and activity type; the apprentice confirms and it goes to the tutor for sign-off. A one-tap share link lets a **site supervisor attest hours from their own phone with no account**; rejected entries can be edited and resubmitted.
**The benefit:** OTJ hours are funding evidence — the thing ESFA audits and colleges chase all year. This makes them verifiable, sourced and forecastable instead of a spreadsheet panic.
**Sell it:** *"Say what you did on the walk back to the van — your off-the-job hours are logged, sent for sign-off, and your gateway forecast updates on the spot."*

### AM2 simulator
**How it works:** Four simulators plus a readiness gauge: an 8-step safe-isolation drill; a testing-sequence simulator with a realistic on-screen multifunction tester (rotary dial, LCD, test buttons) ending in EIC completion; a fault-finding simulator with circuit diagrams and a multimeter; and a knowledge test — plus a timed mock AM2 day and focused drill mode. Every session is scored and kept, and a readiness ring blends simulator scores and mock results into "book it / not yet".
**The benefit:** AM2 attempts cost real money and real nerve. This is deliberate practice on the exact skills, with a data-backed answer to "am I ready?".
**Sell it:** *"Practise the AM2 before you pay for the AM2 — with a working MFT on your phone and a readiness score that tells you when to book."*

### EPA simulator

## 3.2 Inspection & Testing — the certification engine

### The EICR, end to end (the flagship flow)
**How it works:**
1. **Property & supply:** address, supply characteristics, main switch, SPD, earthing — with autocomplete and voice entry on test fields.
2. **Board scanner:** point the camera at the consumer unit. The AI reads the board from photos — circuit positions and labels, device types, curves and ratings, RCD/RCBO presence, main-switch rating, SPD, total ways — and pre-fills the schedule so you only add readings.
3. **Test schedule intelligence:** expected **R1+R2 auto-calculates** from cable type, length and ambient temperature (recorded at ambient — the 1.20 factor is applied to Zs, not R1+R2). One tap fills **maximum Zs for every circuit** from device type/curve/rating — RCD-aware per BS 7671, 80% cold-measured rule applied, and recorded values validated against it. Bottom-sheet **quick-fill panels** bulk-fill RCD details or insulation-resistance thresholds across every blank circuit; any circuit row can be **duplicated** (all device details cloned) for identical circuits. Live validation flags non-compliant values as you type. Auto-save runs on a 1-second debounce with an emergency flush — the back button never eats a schedule.
4. **Observations:** flag a defect, tap for the AI suggestion, and get a proposed classification code (C1/C2/C3/FI), description, remedial recommendation and the specific BS 7671:2018+A4:2026 regulation — accept fields individually or all at once. Photo evidence attaches per observation.
5. **QS review** (if required): submit to your Qualifying Supervisor — see below.
6. **Sign & issue:** unified e-signature (stored once, reused across every schedule and declaration), branded PDF generation, email to the client — oversized photo-heavy PDFs automatically fall back to a secure download link so they never bounce — and one-tap conversion to a quote or invoice for the remedials.
**The benefit:** The EICR is the biggest single time-sink in domestic/landlord work. Every mechanical step — board entry, expected values, max Zs, repeated circuits — is automated, and the judgment steps (coding, remedials) are supported and cited.
**Sell it:** *"Photograph the board, test the circuits, accept the suggested codes — the report writes itself around your readings, and it's on the landlord's email before you've packed the tester away."*

### QS review & certificate governance
**How it works:** Submit any EICR/EIC/Minor Works to a Qualifying Supervisor. The QS sees the full certificate in-app, with AI **pre-flagging dubious test-schedule circuits** for attention. They leave **itemised comments** pinned to specific circuits or observations; the electrician replies in-thread; either side resolves items. On approval the certificate **auto-locks** — its content hash preserved so nothing can drift after counter-signature — with the QS signature embedded in the PDF. Version snapshots keep older/locked copies downloadable. A firm's owner or designated principal QS can review and counter-sign their own certs in one tap from the I&T hub bench.
**The benefit:** For any firm on a competent-person scheme, QS sign-off is the compliance backbone — normally done over email chains and printouts. This is the whole loop, auditable, in the app.
**Sell it:** *"QS sign-off without the email tennis: flagged circuits, pinned comments, in-app replies — and the cert locks itself the moment it's approved."*

### 19 certificate types (the full set)
**How it works:** Beyond EICR/EIC/Minor Works/Testing-Only: Solar PV (MCS-aligned), EV Charging (IET CoP / Section 722), BESS, G98 & G99 commissioning, five BS 5839 fire-alarm certificates (design, installation, commissioning, inspection, modification), Emergency Lighting (BS 5266), Lightning Protection (BS EN 62305), Smoke & CO alarms, and PAT. Plus the on-site paperwork around them: danger notices, isolation certificates, permits, limitation and non-compliance notices, completion notices, warning-label generator and client handouts. All from one selector, all producing branded signed PDFs, all tracked in one gallery with status badges (draft / ready to sign / locked / sent).
**The benefit:** One app covers the whole certification surface of a modern electrical business — including the renewables and fire/life-safety work where the margins are.
**Sell it:** *"Nineteen certificate types in your pocket — from an EICR to a G99 commissioning cert to a BS 5839 fire-alarm handover. If you can be asked for it, Elec-Mate produces it."*

### Certificate expiry tracker
**How it works:** Every issued cert's expiry is tracked, grouped by client, with reminders and conversion metrics. One tap creates the renewal job — client, property and previous cert details pre-populated.
**The benefit:** Your 2021 EICRs are your 2026 revenue. This is a pipeline generator disguised as admin.

## 3.3 Business suite — quoting, invoicing & money

### Quotes, end to end
**How it works:**
1. **Start:** from scratch, or pre-filled from a certificate, site visit, project or materials list. Voice fill completes client fields and adds labour/material lines hands-free.
2. **Build:** guided single-page builder — client, job details (with job-type presets and a "start date to be agreed" toggle), priced line items from your price book / a scanned supplier invoice / an AI cost estimate, per-category markup baked invisibly into customer prices, per-item ± adjustments on a proper decimal keypad, VAT handling, live running total. Auto-saves continuously with draft recovery.
3. **Document type:** one toggle makes it a **Quote** or an **Estimate** — estimates render "guide only, final cost may vary" with relabelled totals, keeping them legally distinct.
4. **Send:** branded PDF (in your company colour, line-item descriptions as clean lists) via email, WhatsApp or native share.
5. **Track:** a status timeline shows sent → viewed (with open counts) → reminded (max three gentle nudges) → accepted. Quotes unanswered 7+ days trigger a one-tap "create follow-up tasks for tomorrow morning" batch.
6. **Acceptance:** the client opens a public page, reviews, signs on their phone — and a **deposit invoice** (your default %, overridable per quote) can be raised automatically with a card-payment link.
7. **Convert:** accepted quote → invoice in one tap, everything carried through. Or one tap → **RAMS**, seeded with the job and client.
**The benefit:** Quoting speed wins work — the first credible quote through the letterbox usually gets the job. And the follow-up automation stops "sent and forgotten" quotes dying quietly.
**Sell it:** *"From site visit to signed acceptance to deposit in the bank — one thread. And the app chases the ones that go quiet, so you don't have to."*

**The benefit:** The gap between "job done" and "money in" is where sole traders bleed. Every step of that gap is compressed or automated.
**Sell it:** *"Invoice from your phone before you've left the drive — card link included — and let the app do the awkward chasing."*

**The benefit:** The whole taught side of an apprenticeship, readable in five-minute chunks on a phone — no "mark as complete" ritual, no losing your place.
**Sell it:** *"Level 2 to HNC on your phone — it even remembers exactly where you stopped reading."*

**Sell it:** *"46+ courses on one subscription — from CSCS prep to leading your first team."*

**How it works:** Every section, quiz and mock earns XP through 10 named levels ("Apprentice" → "Qualified Sparky"); any learning activity feeds a daily streak celebrated on the home hero; a leaderboard ranks by XP/streak/quiz average/achievements with privacy-friendly names and one-tap opt-out; achievements unlock bonus XP.
**The benefit:** Five-minutes-a-day actually happens when there's a streak on the line.
**Sell it:** *"Study like it's a game you're winning — streaks, levels and a leaderboard of UK sparks."*

## 8. Wellbeing

### The hub
**How it works:** A role-aware private space: a 30-second mood tap with haptics and a 7-day heatmap, a wellbeing ring (0–100 from mood, sleep, journalling and consistency), quick resets (guided box-breathing with reduce-motion support, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, a six-technique coping toolkit), a full journal with prompts and export, a sleep tracker framed around safety on site, and mood insights that gently flag when things are dipping.
